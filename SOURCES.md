## Additional Resources and Research Materials

This source list represents a comprehensive compilation of academic, cultural, and popular materials relevant to the study of pocong and Indonesian supernatural beliefs. The interdisciplinary nature of these sources—spanning anthropology, religious studies, psychology, folklore, media studies, and more—reflects the complex, multifaceted nature of the pocong phenomenon.

Researchers should note that Indonesian-language sources often provide more detailed and nuanced information about local beliefs and practices, though English-language anthropological and religious studies literature offers valuable analytical frameworks and comparative perspectives.

The digital age has greatly expanded access to materials, with online databases, digitized archives, and social media platforms providing new sources of information about contemporary manifestations of pocong beliefs. However, traditional ethnographic research, participant observation, and community engagement remain essential for deep understanding of these living cultural traditions.
